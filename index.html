Work/Experience

University of Maryland

Internet of Things Analytics Research Intern• May 2017 - August 2017

Developed a Random Forest machine learning model to classify Internet of Things (IoT) devices by type and brand through analyzing network traffic. I collaborated with others to develop PySpark code using JupyterHub and GitLab. I spearheaded the original codebase using a linear regression model and laid the groundwork for our Random Forest machine learning algorithm.

Worked in conjunction with the Laboratory of Telecommunication Sciences.

Taiwanese American Student Association (TASA)

Internal Vice President• May 2017 - Present

Director of internal bonding, member retention, and the big/little family system. I organize and lead events for members to meet new people and recruit them into their families. For these events, I devise icebreaker games and coordinate logistics to make sure the events run as smooth as possible. I have also developed a search and family tree visualization system for the TASA website which you can find here.

Advanced Cybersecurity Experience for Students (ACES)

Banneker/Key Scholar• August 2016 - Present

ACES is a Cybersecurity focused Honors College that teaches students various cybersecurity concepts including how to use UNIX systems, setting up honeypots, and reverse engineering. The program culminates in a research project using honeypots that students collaborate in groups to set up, design, and maintain. For my group, I developed scripts for extracting data, setting up the honeypots, and recycling the honeypots.

The Banneker/Key scholarship is a full merit scholarship for the top 1% of freshmen entering the University of Maryland, College Park.

Brain Health Alliance

Summer Research Intern• May 2014 - August 2016

Coauthored paper on Web-Enabled Software for Clinical Telegaming Evaluation of Multisensory Integration and Response to Auditory and Visual Stimuli which was presented at the 7th International IEEE EMBS Neural Engineering Conference.

Developed a game that tests multisensory integration with a theoretical application of neural networks using JavaScript, TypeScript, CSS, and HTML in Visual Studio
